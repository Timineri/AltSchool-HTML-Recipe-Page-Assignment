Jollof Pasta

Jollof Pasta is a vibrant, flavorful twist on the beloved West African Jollof rice, replacing the rice with pasta for a hearty, comforting meal. It features pasta tossed in a rich tomato and red pepper sauce, infused with aromatic spices like curry and thyme, and often paired with chicken, beef, or seafood for added protein. Bursting with color and bold flavors, this dish is perfect for family dinners, parties, or quick weeknight meals, bringing the essence of Jollof to the table in a new and delicious way.

Recipe information:

  • Preparation Time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: 1 hour
  • Number of Servings: 4
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 250 g pasta (spaghetti or macaroni)
  • 3 medium tomatoes (or 1 cup blended tomatoes)
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 medium onion (½ for blending, ½ for frying)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 seasoning cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup chicken stock (or water)

Instructions

  1. Boil pasta – Cook pasta in salted boiling water until just al dente. Drain and set aside (save ½ cup pasta water).
  2. Blend sauce – Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, half of the onion, garlic, and scotch bonnet until smooth.
  3. Cook sauce – Heat oil in a pan, sauté the chopped half onion for 2 minutes, then add tomato paste and fry for 2 more minutes.
  4. Add blended mix – Pour in the blended pepper mix and cook on medium heat for 10–15 minutes until reduced and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Season – Add curry powder, thyme, seasoning cube, salt, and chicken stock. Stir well.
  6. Combine pasta – Add the pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. If needed, add a splash of the saved pasta water to loosen.
  7. Optional protein – If using chicken, beef, or shrimp, add them now and stir until well mixed.
  8. Serve hot – Garnish with chopped parsley or spring onions if you like.

Tips

  • For extra flavor, marinate your protein (chicken, beef, or shrimp) with spices before adding it to the sauce.
  • Feel free to add vegetables like peas, carrots, or bell peppers for added nutrition and color.
  • If you prefer a spicier dish, include more scotch bonnet or a dash of cayenne pepper.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days and reheated on the stovetop.
